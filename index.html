Project Overview

Project Title: Green and Healthy Harlem

This project redesigns Harlem Pedestrian Plaza as a greener, healthier, and more comfortable public space. The design focuses on improving shade, seating comfort, planting, and overall public well-being.

Project Goal: To transform the plaza into a green wellness space that supports comfort, relaxation, and healthier daily use through better shade, greenery, and environmental quality.

Essential Question:
How can Harlem Pedestrian Plaza be redesigned to improve comfort, shade, greenery, and public well-being?

Problem Definition

The current plaza lacks shade, greenery, and comfortable seating, making it difficult for people to stay and enjoy the space. Large areas of hard pavement increase heat exposure and reduce environmental comfort.

As a result, the plaza feels less inviting and does not fully support rest, social interaction, or community well-being. This redesign responds to those problems by creating a healthier and more welcoming public environment.

Before vs After

Before

No shade, hard concrete, and limited comfort for visitors.

After

Added trees, shaded seating, and greener spaces for comfort.

Before

Minimal greenery and poor seating conditions reduce use.

After

Improved planting, seating, and wellness-focused atmosphere.

Final Proposal

The final proposal transforms the plaza into a green wellness destination with more shade, comfortable seating, planting areas, and a healthier environment that encourages people to stay, relax, and gather.

Reflection

AI helped generate design ideas and explore different visual possibilities. Human decisions were necessary to choose realistic features, organize the final concept, and make the redesign respond to real user comfort and wellness needs.
